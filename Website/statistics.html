  • Price to Income Ratio is a fundamental measure for apartment purchase affordability, where a lower ratio indicates better affordability.
  • Mortgage as Percentage of Income is a ratio of the actual monthly cost of the mortgage to take-home family income (lower is better).
  • Loan Affordability Index is an inverse of mortgage as percentage of income (higher is better).
  • Price to Rent Ratio is the average cost of ownership divided by the received rent income (if buying to let) or the estimated rent that would be paid if renting (if buying to reside). Lower values suggest that it is better to buy rather than rent, and higher values suggest that it is better to rent rather than buy.
  • Gross Rental Yield is the total yearly gross rent divided by the house price (expressed in percentages). Higher is better.

Why is Housing Solutions Important?

According to danfoss.com, with 180,000 people commuting to cities every day, the need for new housing alternatives is intensifying. In addition to addressing the issue of space, we must ensure that new housing alternatives are appealing, efficient, and environmentally friendly.

As Kent Martinussen, Director of the Danish Architecture Centre, says, finding a sustainable solution to today's urbanization dilemma requires researching unexplored resources in our cities. "People flocking to cities is both a problem and a solution. So, how can we incorporate the problem into the solution? We think wisely. And when I say "think smart," I mean "think sustainably." Sustainable solutions must not only look attractive and be pleasant to live in, but they must also perform."

Thinking smart requires greater collaboration among stakeholders, as well as stronger partnerships between engineers, architects, and contractors in particular. The Urban Rigger student housing project in Copenhagen is one example of how collaboration may improve life in a growing metropolis.
